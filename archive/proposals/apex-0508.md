# APEX combined-perturbation proposal — 2026-05-08

Status: **Adopt combined (c-shift + t-shift) Forward 2 as T3's primary
candidate. Keep temporal-only as a cold-start-safe fallback. Shipped
APEX (cond_diag, b=0) is the baseline.**

This memo replaces the earlier 0508 draft that posed t-shift as a
binary fork from APEX. Today's cross-strategy cosine measurement on
`probe_temporal_shift.py` showed the two perturbation forms are roughly
orthogonal in output-delta space, which makes the question not
"c-shift *or* t-shift" but "is the combination strictly stronger than
either alone".

## Recommendation

1. **Run T3 as a 3-way A/B**: shipped APEX (baseline) ▸ combined-3F
   (primary) ▸ temporal-2F (cold-start-safe alternative). Six epochs
   per lane, same warmstart, identical eval prompt set. Combined-3F
   is "primary" on architectural grounds (orthogonal supervision +
   retained `L_fake` at zero extra compute), **not** on probe wins —
   the 200-step probe showed combined-2F and temporal-2F are
   statistically indistinguishable on `L_mix` descent. The probe
   ruled out instability, not redundancy.
2. **Use Δt = −0.05 wherever t-shift appears.** Cross-strategy cosine
   showed `cond_diag × dt_neg_small` median ≈ 0 (cleaner orthogonal
   complement than `dt_pos_small`'s +0.31), and −Δt avoids the
   upper-clamp artifact at t=0.98 that degenerates ~5% of probe steps.
3. **Combined-3F implementation is one line** in
   `networks/methods/apex.py::APEXAdapter.extra_forwards`: in Forward 2,
   pass `t + Δt` instead of `t`. No new parameters, same memory profile,
   same `L_fake` / fake-trajectory self-distillation as shipped APEX.
4. **If T3 budget is tight, drop combined-3F first.** The probe's
   strongest signal is "all three perturbation forms are stable and
   alive"; the weakest signal is "combined-3F will outperform shipped
   APEX." Shipped APEX vs temporal-2F is the higher-information
   comparison: it tests whether t-shift's structural advantages
   (no `L_fake`, no cold-start risk, −1 forward) cost any quality.

## Why now

Visibility was settled by `apex-0506.md`: with `init_b = 0`, the affine
condition shift clears every attention-divergence gate (real-q KL ≈ 2
nats at init, 0.875 nats after training — q's chase the new K(c_fake)
distribution partway). Remaining APEX failures are **training-time**,
not visibility. The deep-research report's prediction that "perturbation
will degenerate into anchored self-distillation under any hyperparameter
sweep" was conditional on `b ≠ 0` wasting most of the gradient on the
softmax-null token-independent direction; with `b = 0` that prediction
is voided, but the underlying concern — that affine c-shift may be too
narrow a perturbation family to drive a 1-NFE generator — survives.

EMF (Liu et al., arXiv:2602.02571, *Trajectory Consistency for One-Step
Generation on Euler Mean Flows*) raises a structurally different
perturbation: shift `t`, not `c`. Today's probe asks whether t-shift is
in the same operating regime as c-shift, and whether the two are
redundant or complementary.

## Evidence

### 1. Magnitude — t-shift sits in cond_diag's MSE band

`bench/apex/probe_temporal_shift.py` (warmstart `anima-tlora-0507-12`,
8 prompts × 5 t-buckets):

| strategy | mse(med) | snr(med) | ratio vs cond_diag |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| identity | 0.0000 | huge | 0.00 |
| **cond_diag (a=−0.5, b=0 — shipped)** | **0.0199** | 121 | **1.00** |
| cond_signflip (a=−1.0, b=0) | 0.0312 | 82 | 1.57 |
| dt_pos_small (+0.05) | 0.0193 | 103 | 0.97 |
| dt_neg_small (−0.05) | 0.0159 | 116 | 0.80 |
| dt_pos_big (+0.20) | 0.170 | 15 | 8.55 |
| dt_neg_big (−0.20) | 0.210 | 12 | 10.5 |

Two takeaways:
- **At Δt=±0.05, t-shift's `v_fake` MSE is in cond_diag's band**
  (0.80–0.97×). EMF's t-shift idea reaches the same energy budget as
  the shipped APEX adversary.
- **The affine family has a bounded ceiling.** True sign-flip at full
  magnitude (`a=−1.0`) only reaches 1.57× cond_diag — sub-quadratic in
  perturbation magnitude. Doubling `|a|` does not double supervision
  strength. Temporal shift can reach 8–10× cond_diag at Δt=±0.20, but
  SNR collapses to 12–15 there (perturbation comparable to velocity);
  not a useful regime.

### 2. Direction — c-shift and t-shift are roughly orthogonal

The pre-registered question, measured today:
`cos(Δv_a, Δv_b)` where `Δv = v_fake − v_real`, the perturbation
direction in output-delta space.

| pair | cos(med) | IQR [p25, p75] | reading |
|---|---:|---|---|
| `cond_diag × cond_signflip` | **+0.94** | [+0.87, +0.96] | ✓ sanity (same family) |
| `dt_pos_small × dt_neg_small` | **−0.77** | [−0.88, −0.69] | ✓ sanity (opposite t-shifts) |
| `cond_diag × dt_pos_small` | +0.31 | [−0.12, +0.38] | mildly aligned |
| `cond_diag × dt_neg_small` | **−0.01** | [−0.23, +0.47] | **orthogonal** |
| `cond_signflip × dt_pos_small` | +0.36 | [−0.03, +0.41] | mildly aligned |
| `cond_signflip × dt_neg_small` | **−0.01** | [−0.25, +0.42] | **orthogonal** |
| `cond_signflip × dt_pos_big` | +0.45 | [+0.17, +0.51] | aligned (big-Δt drift) |

Sanity rows pin the metric: same-family within ~+1, opposite-direction
within ~−1. Cross-family (c-shift × t-shift) sits at median 0.0–0.36 —
**an order of magnitude lower than within-family redundancy** — with
IQR straddling zero. The two perturbations carry genuinely
complementary information.

Three useful nuances the data shows:
- **−Δt is the cleaner complement to c-shift** (median ≈ 0, vs +0.31
  for +Δt). Use Δt = −0.05.
- **Per-prompt spread is wide.** IQR is ~0.5 wide across prompts; the
  "complementary" finding is in aggregate, not uniform. Some prompts
  will benefit more than others.
- **Orthogonality erodes at big Δt** (`cond_signflip × dt_pos_big`
  rises to +0.45). Stay at small Δt.

### 3. Stability — all three perturbation forms train alive

`bench/apex/probe_temporal_train.py` (warmstart-merged DiT, fresh rank-8
LoRA on 28×{q,kv} cross-attn projections, 0832×1248 bucket, batch=1,
lr=5e-5, rampup=10%, anchor_ratio=0.05). The probe runs **2-forward**
variants of each lane (no `L_fake` / Forward 3) — see
"Probe-vs-T3 lane mapping" below.

200-step 3-way at Δt=−0.05, same seed, same warmstart
(`results/20260508-{1432,1437,1442}-*`):

| metric | temporal-2F | cond_diag-2F | combined-2F |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| L_mix s0–9 | 0.0633 | 0.0626 | 0.0635 |
| L_mix last 20% | 0.0486 | **0.0432** | 0.0495 |
| L_mix descent | −23% | **−31%** | −22% |
| v_fake_div s0–9 | 0.0406 | 0.0128 | **0.0539** |
| v_fake_div last 20% | 0.0357 | **0.0090** | 0.0364 |
| L_fm_obs last 20% | 0.177 | 0.170 | 0.180 |
| L_fm climb | +124% | +115% | +127% |
| grad_norm tail | 0.0335 | **0.0089** | 0.0365 |

(Earlier 100-step temporal Δt=+0.05 run at
`results/20260508-1359-100steps` is consistent with the temporal-2F
column; not reproduced here.)

**All three lanes pass the kill criteria.** No NaN, no oscillation,
v_fake_div in [1e-3, 1e-1] throughout, grad_norm bounded. Step-to-step
spikes (e.g. step 190's synchronized excursion across all three runs)
trace to low-t outliers in the shared seed, not training instability.

Three observations the data *did not* match the prior:

1. **Combined-2F does NOT outperform temporal-2F.** L_mix descent
   −22% vs −23%, statistically indistinguishable. The cross-strategy
   orthogonality measurement was a *necessary* condition for
   combination to add information (signals aren't redundant), but is
   not *sufficient* to predict probe-scale wins. Either the orthogonal
   component lives in directions the rank-8 LoRA delta can't easily
   fit, or 200 steps is too short to differentiate, or the supervision
   form matters less than its existence at all.
2. **Combined-2F's `v_fake_div` is highest at init** (0.054 first10
   vs temporal's 0.041, cond_diag's 0.013) — confirming the cosine
   measurement (perturbations roughly add) — but the larger
   perturbation didn't translate to faster L_mix descent or richer
   gradient signal. Magnitude alone isn't predictive.
3. **cond_diag-2F has 4× quieter gradients** (grad_norm tail 0.009 vs
   ~0.035 for temporal/combined). c-shift is more "fittable" by the
   LoRA delta at probe scale — possibly because affine c-shift mostly
   inverts attention without changing the velocity field's structural
   form, while t-shift moves to a different point on the velocity
   field that LoRA has to actively learn. cond_diag's lower L_mix
   tail (0.043 vs ~0.049) is mathematically tied to its smaller
   v_fake_div (smaller perturbation → T_mix closer to v_data → less to
   descend); it is **not** a quality signal.

#### Probe-vs-T3 lane mapping

The probe's 2-forward variants are not bit-equivalent to T3's
3-forward APEX lanes:

| probe lane (2F) | T3 lane (3F) | difference |
|---|---|---|
| `temporal-2F` | `temporal-2F` | identical (no L_fake either way) |
| `cond_diag-2F` (frozen a, b) | shipped APEX (cond_diag-3F) | T3 trains (a, b) via L_fake; LoRA delta also gets fake-trajectory self-distillation |
| `combined-2F` (frozen a, b) | combined-3F | same — T3 retains L_fake on c_fake-shifted fake trajectory |

So the probe certifies stability for the 2-forward simplification of
each lane. The T3 versions add fake-trajectory self-distillation
(LoRA delta learns to denoise on `x_fake = x_t − t·F_real`), which is
the part of L_fake that actually does work in shipped APEX (since
`(a, b)` drift <3% in 7k steps per `apex.toml`'s old comment).

The takeaway: **the 200-step probe rules out catastrophic instability
in any of the three lanes**, but it does not rank them on T3-scale
quality. Combined-3F's case rests on the architectural argument
(orthogonal supervision + retained L_fake at zero extra compute), not
on probe-measured wins.

## Proposal

### Three lanes for T3

| lane | Forward 2 | L_fake | fwd/step | rationale |
|---|---|---|---:|---|
| **baseline** (APEX-shipped) | `anima(x_t, t, c_fake)` | yes | 3 | published method; control |
| **combined-3F** (primary) | `anima(x_t, t+Δt, c_fake)` | yes | **3** | adds orthogonal supervision at same compute |
| **temporal-2F** (cheap) | `anima(x_t, t+Δt, c)` | no | **2** | −1 forward, no cold-start risk, loses fake-trajectory self-distillation |

`c_fake = ConditionShift(c)` with shipped `mode="diag", init_a=−0.5,
init_b=0`. Δt = −0.05 throughout.

### Why combined-3F is the primary candidate

The case is *architectural*, not probe-measured. The 200-step probe
showed combined-2F and temporal-2F descend `L_mix` at indistinguishable
rates; orthogonality didn't translate to faster supervision at probe
scale. The reasons to still lead with combined-3F:

- **Cross-strategy cosine measurement** (the necessary condition):
  c-shift and t-shift point in roughly orthogonal directions in
  output-delta space, so combining is not redundant. The probe ran
  combined-*2F* (no L_fake); the orthogonality justification still
  holds for combined-3F because the perturbation form is unchanged.
- **Same compute as shipped APEX**: 3 forwards/step. No memory profile
  change vs the live trainer; `blocks_to_swap = 0` is already forced.
  If combined-3F has *any* advantage at T3 scale, it's free relative
  to shipped APEX.
- **Retains `L_fake` / fake-trajectory self-distillation.** Audit of
  `apex.py:427` confirms L_fake's gradient flows to **both** `(a, b)`
  and the LoRA delta. Per `apex.toml`'s old comment, `(a, b)` drift
  <3% in 7k steps — so most of L_fake's practical effect is the LoRA
  delta learning to denoise on the model's own one-step output
  trajectory `x_fake = x_t − t · F_real`. That signal is the part of
  APEX the probe didn't measure (probe is 2-forward).
- **Theoretical purity is unchanged from shipped APEX**: c_fake and
  L_fake are both intact, so Section 3.3's Fisher-divergence-aligned
  gradient interpretation (with `w ≡ 1`) carries over. T-shift here is
  additive supervision in Forward 2's target, not a replacement for
  c_fake's role as the independent score estimator.

The honest framing: combined-3F is a low-risk superset of shipped APEX
(retains everything, adds an orthogonal perturbation at zero extra
cost). It's primary because it dominates shipped APEX in expectation,
not because the probe showed it dominates temporal-2F.

### Why keep temporal-2F as a fallback lane

- **Cold-start safety.** APEX cold-start failure (Phase 0: −48% NFE=1
  W1 vs cold-start FM) traces to L_fake on a random
  `x_fake = x_t − t · F_θ(random)` poisoning the network through shared
  weights. Temporal-2F drops L_fake entirely → this failure mode is
  structurally removed. Useful if combined-3F has a stability problem
  the probe didn't catch.
- **Compute economy.** 2 forwards/step → 33% faster training, more
  VRAM headroom for batch>1 or higher resolution. Real win if NFE=1
  quality is comparable.
- **Cost it pays**: loses fake-trajectory self-distillation. Live
  trainer (`apex/v_fake_divergence`) settling from 2.09 nats at init
  to 0.875 nats after training is the trace of that self-distillation
  in the shipped lane — temporal-2F has no equivalent adaptation
  mechanism, so its v_fake target is a fixed Δt offset that doesn't
  co-evolve with the model.

## Implementation if T3 picks combined-3F

Single change in `networks/methods/apex.py::APEXAdapter.extra_forwards`,
the Forward 2 block (currently lines 304-311):

```diff
 with torch.no_grad():
+    apex_dt = float(getattr(args, "apex_dt", 0.0) or 0.0)
+    if apex_dt != 0.0:
+        t_eff = (timesteps.float() + apex_dt).clamp(0.02, 0.98).to(timesteps.dtype)
+    else:
+        t_eff = timesteps
     v_fake_sg = anima(
         noisy_model_input,
-        timesteps,
+        t_eff,
         c_fake.detach(),
         padding_mask=padding_mask,
         **kw,
     )
```

Plus:
- `apex_dt = -0.05` in `configs/methods/apex.toml` (or a new
  `configs/methods/apex_combined.toml` to avoid disturbing shipped).
- New CLI arg `--apex_dt` in `library/anima/training.py` (mirrors
  existing `--apex_lambda*` knobs).
- Telemetry: `apex/v_fake_divergence` already covers this. Add
  `apex/dt_clamp_frac` to track how often `t_eff` hits the 0.02/0.98
  clamps (the upper clamp at +Δt is the artifact that degenerated 5%
  of probe steps; with −Δt the lower clamp is rarely hit because
  t ~ U(0.02, 0.98) puts only ~5% of mass in [0.02, 0.07]).

If T3 picks temporal-2F instead, the implementation is the existing
`probe_temporal_train.py` blueprint scaled up: drop ConditionShift
attachment, drop Forward 3, drop L_fake from the loss aggregator.
Larger code change but mechanically simple.

## Risks and what this does not prove

The cross-strategy cosine measurement and 100-step probe rule out
*direction-redundancy* and *training-time degeneracy*. They do **not**
rule out:

- **Direction equivalence with shipped APEX.** Even if combined-3F
  trains stably and produces orthogonal supervision in output space,
  its gradient on the LoRA delta may converge to a different fixed
  point than shipped APEX. Two stable training runs can produce
  different sample distributions. T3's NFE=1 GenEval / W1 / FID is
  the only direct test.
- **Per-prompt non-uniformity.** Cross-strategy cosine IQR is ~0.5
  wide; for some prompts c-shift and t-shift are aligned (combination
  is just denoising the same signal), for others they're mildly
  opposing (partial cancellation). T3 should report per-prompt
  variance, not just aggregate scores.
- **Sample quality vs the modulation-guidance / spectrum stack.** APEX
  is one of several inference-time accelerators for Anima; its win
  condition is "1-NFE quality competitive with 4-NFE shipped + 4×
  faster". Combined-3F doesn't change this calculus — it changes the
  training-time supervision form, not inference.

## Open questions for after T3

- Does the `cond_signflip × dt_pos_big` cosine alignment (+0.45) at
  `Δt = +0.20` mean that *aggressive* combined-perturbation training
  would collapse the orthogonality? Probe at multiple Δt magnitudes
  if combined-3F passes.
- Is the per-prompt variance in cross-strategy cosine correlated with
  caption length / aspect ratio / domain? If so, a calibrator-style
  per-prompt blend would be the next refinement (cf. DCW v4's online
  observation + prompt fusion).
- Should the inner λ schedule be different per-lane? Combined-3F's
  v_fake target is structurally richer; smaller λ may suffice.

## What this does NOT change

- `apex_condition_shift_init_b = 0.0` stays. 0506's finding holds.
- `blocks_to_swap = 0` stays method-forced. Multi-forward pattern.
- Warm-start (`network_weights` + `dim_from_weights = true`) stays
  mandatory. Cold-start failure mode is unchanged.
